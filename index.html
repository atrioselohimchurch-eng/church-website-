Our Story

About Atrios Elohim Church

Who We Are

A Community Rooted
in Scripture & Love

Atrios Elohim Church is a multigenerational, multicultural community of faith located at 6 Court Street in Flemington, NJ. We are a congregation committed to worship, discipleship, and authentic service — building lives on the unshakeable foundation of God's Word.

Our Mission

“To glorify God by making disciples of all nations, cultivating a community rooted in Scripture, prayer, and authentic love.”

Everything we do at Atrios Elohim Church flows from this mission. We are not simply a gathering place — we are a sending community. We gather on Sundays to worship, to be filled with God's Word, and to be sharpened by the Holy Spirit. But the moment the service ends, the mission begins.

Making disciples means more than teaching people facts about the Bible. It means walking alongside people through every season of life — celebrating their victories, sitting with them in their pain, and calling out the greatness God has placed inside of them.

We are a multicultural church by conviction, not by accident. We believe the Kingdom of God looks like every tribe, tongue, and nation — and we strive every day to reflect that reality right here in Flemington, NJ.

Our Vision

“A thriving, multigenerational church where every person encounters the living God, is transformed by the Gospel, and is sent to serve their community and the world.”

We are building something that will outlast any of us. Our vision is not simply for today — it is for the next generation and the generation after that. We are planting seeds of faith in Flemington, NJ that we believe will bear fruit for decades to come.

“Thriving” means more than growing in numbers. It means growing in depth — in prayer, in love, in generosity, in boldness. We want to be a church where the presence of God is so tangible that lives are healed, families are restored, and the broken are made whole.

“Sent” is the word that defines what makes us different. Every person who comes through our doors will eventually be equipped and released — into their workplace, their neighbourhood, their family, and yes, to the nations. That is the heartbeat of Atrios Elohim Church.

How We Got Here

Our Story

Atrios Elohim Church was born out of a simple but radical conviction — that the church of Jesus Christ was never meant to be a spectator sport. From the very beginning, Apostol Edwin Colop gathered a small group of believers in Flemington, NJ with one purpose: to encounter God together and then go change the world.

What began as a small gathering quickly became a movement. People from different backgrounds, different cultures, and different life experiences found something rare here — a community where they were not just welcomed but truly known. A place where worship was real, preaching was honest, and love was demonstrated not just proclaimed.

Today, Atrios Elohim Church stands as a testament to what God can do with a faithful remnant. We have seen baptisms in rivers, street ministry that has fed hundreds, chaplains commissioned to serve their communities, and families transformed by the power of the Gospel. And we believe the best is still ahead.

What We Stand On

Our Theology

Atrios Elohim Church is a Spirit-filled, evangelical church. Our theology is rooted in the full authority of Scripture and the apostolic-prophetic heritage of the Christian faith. We hold to historic, orthodox Christianity while embracing the present-day work of the Holy Spirit.

Core Theological Positions
Biblical Authority

We affirm the Bible as the inspired, inerrant Word of God — the final authority for all matters of faith, conduct, and doctrine. Every sermon, every teaching, every decision flows from Scripture.

Apostolic-Prophetic Ministry

We believe in the five-fold ministry of Ephesians 4:11 — apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers — as gifts given by Christ for the equipping and maturing of the Church in every generation.

Baptism in the Holy Spirit

We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit as a distinct experience available to all believers, accompanied by spiritual gifts including speaking in tongues, prophecy, healing, and miracles — all for the purpose of advancing God's Kingdom.

The Great Commission

We believe every believer is called to make disciples of all nations (Matthew 28:19-20). Missions, evangelism, and church planting are not optional extras — they are the heartbeat of what it means to follow Jesus.

Divine Healing

We believe healing is provided in the atonement of Christ and that God still heals today — physically, emotionally, and spiritually. We regularly pray for the sick in every service and believe in the miraculous power of God.

First Time Here?

What to Expect
When You Visit

We know visiting a new church can feel like a big step. We want to make it as easy and comfortable as possible. Here is everything you need to know before you walk through our doors.

No Dress Code

Come exactly as you are. You will see people in suits and people in jeans — both are equally welcome. God looks at your heart, and so do we.

Authentic Worship

Our worship is real, passionate, and full of the Holy Spirit. Come ready to engage — whether that means raising your hands, sitting quietly, or somewhere in between. There is no performance here, just genuine praise.

Biblical, Relevant Preaching

Apostol Edwin Colop preaches directly from Scripture with clarity, authority, and practical application for your everyday life. You will leave with something to hold onto. Services typically run 90 minutes to 2 hours.

A Welcoming Community

From the moment you walk in, our hospitality team is ready to greet you, answer your questions, and help you feel at home. You are not just a visitor — you are family from the moment you step through the door.

Children Are Welcome

We love kids at Atrios Elohim Church. Children are welcome in the main service. Ask our hospitality team about what children's programming is currently available for your child's age group.

Prayer Ministry Available

Our prayer team is available at every service to pray with you personally. Whether you are carrying a burden, seeking healing, or simply want someone to stand with you in prayer — we are here. No request is too big or too small.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

Do I need to be a Christian to attend?
Absolutely not. Everyone is welcome at Atrios Elohim Church — regardless of where you are on your spiritual journey. Whether you are a long-time believer, a skeptic, or someone who has never stepped inside a church before, you are welcome here. We believe God meets people right where they are.
What language are the services in?
Our services are conducted primarily in Spanish, with English translation available. As a multicultural congregation, we strive to make every person feel at home regardless of their language background. If you have any specific accessibility needs, please contact us before your visit.
How long are the services?
Sunday morning services typically last between 90 minutes and 2 hours. Wednesday Prayer Night and Friday services vary but generally run 1.5 to 2 hours. We never rush an encounter with God — but we also respect your time.
Is there parking available?
Street parking is available around 6 Court Street in Flemington. We recommend arriving a few minutes early on Sunday to find a spot. If you need any special parking assistance, please contact us and we will do our best to accommodate you.
Do you take up an offering during the service?
Yes, we do receive a voluntary offering as part of our Sunday service. As a first-time guest you are our invited guest and there is absolutely no obligation to give. Giving is an act of worship for members and regular attenders — never a requirement for visitors.
I'm going through a difficult time. Can the church help?
Yes — and we want to. At Atrios Elohim Church, we believe the church is most needed when life is hardest. Our prayer team is available at every service. You can also reach out to us directly via phone or email and we will connect you with the right support. You do not have to face this alone.
